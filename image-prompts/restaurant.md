# Restaurant — Image Prompts

## Setting vocabulary
The restaurant vertical spans three distinct sub-spaces: an intimate evening dining room dressed in warm walnut, oxblood leather, and moody pendant light; a cocktail bar lined with blackened steel, marble counter, and brass tap fixtures; and a sun-drenched outdoor patio with string lights overhead, terracotta tile underfoot, and lightweight furniture arranged around bistro tables. Each product prompt anchors the piece in the sub-space that suits it — bar stools live at the bar counter, booths claim the dining banquette wall, outdoor chairs occupy the patio terrace.

## Plain-background style
A muted bone or soft warm-gray seamless studio backdrop — no floor seam visible, walls dissolving gently into shadow at the edges. Diffuse overhead softbox light eliminates hard shadows, supplemented by twin rim-lights at roughly 45-degree angles that carve a clean silhouette separation between the furniture and the background. The tone is quiet luxury: Aesop minimal, Apple product photography applied to commercial hospitality furniture.

## Furniture types to add
**Host Stand / Podium** — every restaurant entrance needs one; anchors the greeting zone and completes a full front-of-house story.
**Server Station / Side Station** — essential back-of-house piece that hospitality buyers always specify alongside seating.
**Banquette Bench (wall-mounted)** — runs along a dining-room wall, pairing with the dining chairs and tables already in the collection.
**High-Top Communal Table** — pairs with the bar stools; a natural upsell for casual dining and brewery-style venues.
**Waiting Bench (lobby/entrance)** — compact upholstered bench for the entrance queue; common in any mid-volume restaurant.
**Outdoor Lounge / Club Chair** — low-slung companion to the patio tables; differentiates the patio zone from pure dining.
**Wine Display Cabinet / Bottle Rack** — décor-forward storage piece; F&B buyers routinely specify it with dining furniture.
**Sommelier Cart / Service Trolley** — rolling service piece; small footprint, high visual interest in editorial shots.
**Private Dining Room Chair** — slightly elevated upholstered version of the dining chair family, sold into PDR packages.
**Bar Back Shelving Unit** — behind-bar storage that photographers love as a background layer in bar-scene shots.
