# Residential — Image Prompts

> **Note:** DMD's current XML catalog contains ZERO residential products. DMD markets a residential vertical in their site copy / services page, so this file is a placeholder with setting vocabulary and a "Furniture types to add" backlog for future product additions. When residential products are added to `public/DMD_Website.xml`, each one gets 7 Nano Banana prompts following the same slot spec as other verticals (see `README.md`).

## Setting vocabulary

Residential photography anchors in three canonical interiors: a modern living room with floor-to-ceiling windows, natural afternoon light raking across soft linen and wool textiles, and pale oak flooring; a primary bedroom with warm walnut tones, layered linen and cotton bedding in bone and terracotta, and directional bedside lighting that reads intimate without being dim; and a dining room with woven-rattan pendants, an oak-top table, and plaster walls that photograph as an almost-white neutral. Outdoor shots belong on a cedar-decked patio with mature planters and rattan accents — overcast Pacific Northwest light or open shade only, never harsh midday sun. Each setting must be empty and uninhabited, styled with a single hero piece and minimal supporting props (a book, a ceramic, a throw) that reinforce the room story without competing with the product.

## Plain-background style

Plain-background shots use a seamless bone or light warm-gray paper sweep — never cold studio white, which reads clinical against wood and fabric. Lighting is a large overhead softbox (tent-style) diffused through a half-stop of frost gel, with a low-power fill card on the shadow side to open detail without eliminating depth. The result is the same soft-shadow, color-accurate look used across DMD's hospitality and healthcare plain-bg shots, keeping residential catalog images consistent with the rest of the site even before room-set photography is produced.

## Furniture types to add (full residential product backlog)

DMD should build out the following products to compete in residential. Organize by room:

### Living Room (AT LEAST 8 items)

- **Modular 3-piece sectional with reversible chaise** — DMD's custom-upholstery pitch transfers directly here; a reversible chaise lets buyers flip the configuration, which is the single most common objection in residential sofa sales and a natural demo for DMD's "built to your spec" positioning.
- **Accent armchair (swivel base, performance fabric)** — pairs with the sectional for a two-SKU living-room bundle; the swivel base is a high-search, differentiating feature that shows up in Architectural Digest editorial constantly.
- **Solid wood coffee table with storage shelf** — a lower table with an open shelf below reads as both functional and photographable from above and at eye level, two of the seven prompt slots.
- **Floating media console / TV credenza** — one of the highest-volume residential furniture searches; DMD's custom-sizing capability is the obvious upsell ("specify your TV width, we size the bay").
- **Bookcase / open shelving unit (floor-to-ceiling)** — works as both standalone product and room-set background architecture; five-shelf models in walnut or white oak anchor the home-office and living-room settings simultaneously.
- **Upholstered ottoman with tray top** — dual-function (footrest + coffee table) with a strong gifting angle; small enough to photograph in full on a plain background, which keeps prompt production cheap for launch.
- **Side / end table (hairpin or solid leg variants)** — low price point, high accessory search volume; positions DMD as a full-room supplier rather than big-piece-only.
- **Accent console / sofa table** — sits behind a floating sofa or against an entry wall; the long-narrow silhouette is a natural hero for the split-view slot-0/slot-1 technique.
- **Floor lamp (arc, tripod, or task variants)** — lighting is the fastest way to complete a room set without adding a second furniture SKU; also surfaces DMD in "floor lamp" search intent that pure furniture brands miss.

### Bedroom (AT LEAST 8 items)

- **Platform bed frame (upholstered headboard, solid-wood base)** — the anchor bedroom SKU; an upholstered headboard lets DMD show fabric swatches at the PDP level, mirroring the custom-upholstery workflow already in place for hospitality.
- **Storage bed (drawers under base)** — addresses the #1 bedroom objection (lack of storage) and justifies a premium price point; two-drawer and four-drawer variants are effectively separate SKUs with minimal incremental photography.
- **Nightstand / bedside table (2-drawer)** — almost always sold in pairs, which doubles average order value with zero additional photography per unit.
- **6-drawer dresser (solid wood or mixed media)** — high-search, high-margin bedroom staple; a tall and wide variant are visually distinct enough to warrant separate photography sessions.
- **Wardrobe / armoire (for rooms without built-in closets)** — underserved SKU category in the mid-range market; DMD's custom-sizing story is strongest here because buyers almost always have an awkward alcove.
- **Upholstered bench (end-of-bed)** — pairs with the platform bed frame naturally; a 48" and 60" variant covers queen and king footboards.
- **Vanity table with mirror** — high Instagram and Pinterest search volume; the mirror creates a natural reflective element that makes plain-background prompt composition more dynamic.
- **Accent chair / reading chair (for bedroom corner)** — positions DMD as a full-bedroom supplier; the isolated corner vignette is one of the most pinned bedroom images on any inspiration platform.
- **Bunk bed / loft bed (crossover with Kids)** — appears in both bedroom and kids search results; a twin-over-full variant addresses the widest age range and the largest floor-plan constraints.

### Dining Room (AT LEAST 6 items)

- **Solid wood dining table (rectangular, extendable leaf)** — the primary dining SKU; an extendable leaf variant justifies two hero shots (compact and extended) for five of the seven prompt slots.
- **Upholstered dining chair (set of 4, in performance fabric)** — the highest-margin dining accessory; fabric customization is the obvious DMD upsell.
- **Bench seat (matches dining table)** — sold as a table companion for casual-dining and family households; the long silhouette photographs well in the split-view slot-0/slot-1 format.
- **Bar-height dining table + counter stools** — addresses a separate kitchen-island and breakfast-nook buyer persona that the standard-height table misses.
- **Sideboard / buffet cabinet** — dining-room storage that also functions as a display surface; woven-front or fluted-door variants align with the Kinfolk Home aesthetic DMD wants to own.
- **China cabinet / display hutch** — higher AOV than sideboard alone; the glass-front doors create transparency that requires a separate lighting setup — strong differentiator for prompt variety.
- **Rattan or cane dining chairs** — a distinct material story from the upholstered chair; lets DMD capture "rattan dining chair" search intent and signals design range.

### Home Office (AT LEAST 5 items)

- **Standing desk / sit-stand desk (solid top, adjustable legs)** — the dominant home-office search term since 2020; DMD's custom-sizing pitch is strongest here because buyers almost always need a non-standard width.
- **Executive desk (L-shape or returns)** — addresses the dedicated home-office buyer who needs corner coverage; solid wood with cable management routing is the differentiating spec to call out.
- **Ergonomic task chair** — high-search, high-margin; even a single upholstered task chair in a performance fabric lets DMD stay consistent with the custom-upholstery workflow.
- **Bookcase / credenza combo (desk-height)** — matches the desk as a lateral filing / display unit; sold as a set for higher AOV, or individually for buyers who already have a desk.
- **Filing cabinet (2- or 4-drawer, in wood or powder-coat steel)** — underserved in the mid-design-market; a solid walnut or white oak finish moves it from "office supply" to "furniture" and opens editorial placement.
- **Monitor arm / desk riser (accessory)** — not furniture, but appears in "home office setup" searches constantly; positions DMD as a full-workspace supplier and creates natural bundle opportunities.

### Outdoor / Patio (AT LEAST 5 items)

- **Modular outdoor sectional (all-weather wicker, Sunbrella cushions)** — the outdoor counterpart to the indoor modular sectional; Sunbrella fabric lets DMD reuse the custom-upholstery story with a weather-resistant angle.
- **Outdoor dining table + chairs set (teak or powder-coat aluminum)** — teak grain photographs beautifully in open shade and reads as premium without looking commercial; 6-seat and 8-seat variants are visually distinct SKUs.
- **Outdoor lounge chair / chaise longue** — high search volume in spring/summer; the reclined silhouette is one of the few furniture forms that actually benefits from an outdoor setting shot on an actual patio rather than a studio sweep.
- **Outdoor coffee table (concrete top or teak slab)** — anchors the seating vignette; a concrete or live-edge top is a strong material-story differentiator from mass-market outdoor furniture.
- **Pergola / shade structure (flat-pack or custom)** — positions DMD in outdoor architecture, not just furniture; the structural element creates a natural framing device for the outdoor photography setting.
- **Outdoor bar cart / serving trolley** — low price point, high gifting volume; the caster wheels and slatted shelf photograph well at the plain-background format because the negative space reads as intentional.

### Kids / Nursery (AT LEAST 4 items)

- **Convertible crib-to-toddler-bed** — the highest-AOV nursery SKU because it ships as two products over the child's lifespan; DMD's custom-finish options (white, natural, walnut) let parents match existing bedroom furniture.
- **Bunk bed (twin-over-full, with safety rail)** — crossover with the bedroom category; the structural complexity is a natural showcase for DMD's build quality and differentiates from flat-pack alternatives.
- **Kids' study desk + hutch** — growing search category as hybrid schooling continues; the integrated hutch keeps books and supplies off the desk surface, which is the main parent objection in this category.
- **Low-profile bookcase / toy storage unit** — child-height shelving with open cubbies is the most-pinned nursery product on Pinterest after cribs; the colorful bin inserts create natural lifestyle photography opportunity without adding people to the shot.
- **Activity table + chair set (toddler height)** — durable top with a non-toxic finish is the key spec; positions DMD in the "heirloom-quality kids furniture" niche rather than competing on price with IKEA.

### Entryway / Mudroom (AT LEAST 3 items)

- **Entryway bench with shoe storage (open cubby or drawer base)** — the first piece of furniture a visitor sees; a natural-wood top with painted base is the most editorial-friendly colorway and aligns with the Dwell home-tour aesthetic DMD references.
- **Coat rack / hall tree (freestanding, with mirror panel)** — a mirror panel in a narrow entryway doubles the apparent depth of the shot — a compositional advantage for the split-view prompt slots without requiring a wide room set.
- **Console table (slim-profile, under 14" deep)** — the entryway's primary display surface; at under 14" deep it passes through most interior doorways fully assembled, which is a practical differentiator worth calling out in product copy alongside image prompts.
- **Wall-mounted key-and-mail organizer (with hooks and ledge)** — accessory-level price point but appears in entryway search clusters; positions DMD in the "entry system" category where AOV is driven by bundles rather than single high-ticket pieces.

## Nano Banana prompt rules reminder

When residential products are added to `public/DMD_Website.xml`, follow the same rules as other verticals:

- **5-component formula:** SUBJECT + ACTION + LOCATION/CONTEXT + COMPOSITION + STYLE
- **100–200 words per prompt,** written as narrative paragraphs (not bulleted specs)
- **No banned keywords:** "photorealistic", "4K", "8K", "ultra HD", "masterpiece", "highly detailed", "hyperrealistic", "best quality", "cinematic lighting", "octane render", "ray tracing"
- **No negative prompts;** use positive framing only — e.g., "empty, uninhabited space" not "no people"
- **Prestigious anchors:** "Architectural Digest home feature", "Dwell home tour", "Kinfolk Home", "Wallpaper* residential editorial", "House Beautiful room of the year"
- **Split-view technique** for slots 0 + 1: a single wide frame that shows the product in its room-set context on one side and a close-up material/detail crop on the other
- **Setting must match product:** living-room pieces → living-room setting; bedroom pieces → bedroom setting; outdoor pieces → outdoor patio setting. No mixing.
- **No people in any image;** frame as "empty, uninhabited" interior or exterior
- **No measurements or rulers** visible in any shot
- **Product identity** held consistent across slots 0–3; one controlled variation (angle, time of day, accent prop) permitted in slots 4–6
- **Naming convention:** `{slug}-slot{N}-{type}.webp` (e.g., `platform-bed-walnut-slot0-split.webp`)
