# Hospital — Image Prompts

## Setting vocabulary

Modern hospital interiors have moved decisively toward a hospitality-grade aesthetic: patient rooms feature wood-look LVT flooring, matte white or warm greige walls, LED troffer indirect lighting that casts no harsh shadows, and a biophilic plant on the windowsill behind frosted privacy film. Waiting areas use grouped upholstered seating arranged around low side tables, acoustic ceiling panels in soft off-white, and gallery-style framed prints that read calm rather than clinical. Consultation rooms lean into warm wood cabinetry, a wall-mounted handwash station in the background, and a pair of padded chairs across a compact desk — closer to a private GP practice than an old-fashioned hospital office.

## Plain-background style

A seamless seamless sweep in bone white or soft sage green — no texture, no gradient — lit by softbox diffused light from camera-left, a secondary fill from the right, and a rim light behind to lift the piece cleanly off the background. Shadows are minimal and directional; the only thing competing for attention is the product itself.

## Furniture types present / suggested for expansion

Patient Room: bedside cabinets (double-drawer, lockable, single-drawer), overbed tables (adjustable, fixed, rolling), patient recliners (electric, manual), lounge chairs (upholstered, contoured, compact). Waiting Area: magazine/side tables (glass, metal, wood), reception desks (curved, glass-top, straight), waiting chairs (linked beam, plastic, upholstered). Additional candidates for future shoots: nurse station / clinician workstation, bariatric-rated IV infusion chair with side table, blood-draw phlebotomy chair, exam-room rolling stool, patient wardrobe/locker, visitor sleeper companion chair, pediatric waiting chairs, emergency department triage bench, pharmacy waiting bench, mother's room glider, discharge lounge chair, rehab therapy seating, cafeteria banquette, staff breakroom lounge, outdoor healing-garden bench.
