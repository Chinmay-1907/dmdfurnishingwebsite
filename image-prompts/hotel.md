# Hotel — Image Prompts

## Setting vocabulary
The canonical hotel setting spans four distinct zones: a boutique lobby with warm oak floors, textured plaster walls, and low pendant lighting casting amber pools across a reception desk in the background; guestrooms with muted linen textiles, blackout drapes half-drawn to reveal soft morning light, and reading nooks framed by architectural bedside panels; a breakfast area bathed in natural morning light through full-height glazing, with herringbone tile floors and rattan pendant clusters; and a restaurant or lounge with moody brass accents, leather banquettes, and candlelit evening warmth. All settings are empty and uninhabited — no figures, no body parts, no staffed counters.

## Plain-background style
Every plain-background shot places the product on a seamless surface where walls and floor share a single warm neutral tone — taupe, bone, or warm gray — with no visible horizon line. Light comes from a large diffuse overhead source reinforced by a soft tent wrap, creating gentle rim separation that lifts the product away from the ground plane. A subtle fill card opposite the key source prevents deep shadows. The overall mood reads like an Apple product photography suite applied to furniture: calm, editorial, and entirely focused on form.

## Furniture types to add
- **Guestroom Bench (foot-of-bed)** — Every hotel guestroom needs a seat at the foot of the bed for dressing; DMD's catalog has a luggage bench but no upholstered guestroom bench in a coordinating fabric family.
- **Valet Stand / Suit Valet** — Business and boutique hotels expect a freestanding valet stand for garment hanging; absent from the current lineup despite being a high-margin, simple piece.
- **Concierge / Front Desk Counter** — The lobby front desk is the first impression of every hotel; a dedicated counter unit with cable management and branding panel insets belongs in DMD's commercial offering.
- **Lobby Coffee Table** — Low-profile coffee tables to pair with the existing arm chairs and sofas in the lobby zone; currently missing a dedicated lobby table SKU.
- **Pool / Patio Lounge Chair** — Outdoor-rated chaise lounges for hotel pool decks and terraces represent a natural extension of the hospitality line and a high-volume hospitality spec.
- **Minibar / In-Room Storage Cabinet** — A compact freestanding or built-in minibar cabinet with lock provision is a standard guestroom FF&E specification that DMD does not currently list.
- **Spa / Wellness Waiting Bench** — Upholstered bench seating for hotel spa reception areas; slimmer profile than a lobby bench, often requiring moisture-friendly fabric.
- **Breakfast Buffet High Stool / Counter Stool** — Counter-height seating to pair with the buffet counter and food service cabinet lineup for grab-and-go breakfast formats common in select-service hotels.
- **Hotel Room Wardrobe / Closet Unit** — A freestanding wardrobe with mirror, hanging rail, and safe provision is a core guestroom FF&E item missing from the catalog.
- **Bellhop / Luggage Cart** — A styled metal luggage cart with wood shelf inserts serves both function and brand expression in boutique lobbies.
