Common Questions

What buyers ask before starting a furniture project.

What is FF&E and why does it matter for hospitality projects?

FF&E stands for Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment: the movable items in a commercial space such as beds, desks, chairs, lighting, and accessories that are not permanently attached to the structure. For hotels, restaurants, and offices, FF&E typically represents 15 to 25 percent of total construction costs and directly shapes guest experience and brand consistency. The American Hotel & Lodging Association (AHLA) offers industry guidance on hospitality standards and capital planning.

What types of commercial spaces does DMD Furnishing serve?

DMD Furnishing serves hotels, restaurants, corporate offices, educational facilities, healthcare-adjacent spaces, and franchise renovation programs. We carry 174+ products across 7 market categories, all built for commercial-grade durability.

How does DMD’s custom furniture process work?

Every project starts with a consultation to lock in scope, budget, and timeline. We then manage design specs, manufacturing, delivery, and installation, all under one project manager from first call to final punch list.

What is value engineering in commercial furniture?

Value engineering means analyzing materials, construction methods, and design details to cut cost without sacrificing quality or visual intent. At DMD, it’s standard on every bid. We find where smarter material choices and manufacturing methods can hit your budget while keeping the design intact.

How much does custom hospitality furniture cost?

It depends on scope, materials, room count, and customization. A 20-room motel refresh and a 300-key hotel renovation have very different budgets. We value-engineer every project to hit your number. Schedule a call and we’ll give you a realistic range.

Can DMD handle both small renovations and large-scale hotel projects?

Yes. From a single lobby refresh to a 300-room hotel renovation or phased franchise rollout. Our hybrid manufacturing model handles speed-sensitive small jobs and high-volume programs equally well.

Where is DMD Furnishing based and what areas do you serve?

DMD Furnishing is headquartered at 56 Leonard St, Unit 5, Foxboro, MA 02035. We serve commercial clients and hospitality operators nationwide, with project experience spanning hotels, restaurants, and institutional spaces across the United States.
