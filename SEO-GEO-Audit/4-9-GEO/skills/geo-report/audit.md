# Skill: geo-report (GEO Narrative Report) — dmdredesign.netlify.app
**Date:** 2026-04-09 | **Status:** COMPLETE

## Summary
Formatted narrative report on DMD Furnishing's readiness for AI-powered search engines. The site is technically well-prepared for generative search (SSR, AI crawlers allowed, llms.txt present) but has zero external authority signals, making it invisible to AI systems that rely on cross-source entity verification before generating citations.

## Narrative Assessment

DMD Furnishing's website has taken the right technical steps for AI search readiness. The Next.js 15 implementation delivers server-rendered HTML that AI crawlers can fully parse. The robots.txt explicitly welcomes GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and other AI crawlers. An llms.txt file provides structured business context specifically designed for AI consumption.

However, technical access alone does not drive AI citations. When AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude generate responses about hospitality furniture, they draw from entities they can verify across multiple authoritative sources. DMD Furnishing currently has zero external verification:

- **No Wikipedia or Wikidata entry** — the primary knowledge base for AI entity resolution
- **No YouTube presence** — a major source AI systems use for business verification
- **LinkedIn company page returns 404** — one of the few social signals that was configured is broken
- **No Reddit mentions** — community discussions are a strong AI citation signal
- **No industry directory listings** — HospitalityNet, Hotel Business, and similar directories are absent
- **No Google Business Profile** — fundamental for local entity recognition

The result is a technically perfect door that leads to an empty room. AI crawlers can access the site but find no external corroboration to justify citing DMD in generated responses.

## Key Findings
- Technical AI readiness is in the top tier (88-100/100) — this is a genuine competitive advantage
- Brand authority is critically low (8-18/100) — the single biggest blocker to AI visibility
- Content quality is moderate — present but not differentiated enough to trigger citation over competitors
- The gap between technical readiness and authority represents the highest-ROI opportunity

## Recommendations
- Treat external entity building as the top priority — no amount of on-site optimization will compensate for zero external presence
- Create a structured 90-day entity-building plan starting with Google Business Profile, LinkedIn, and Wikidata
- Develop "citability-first" content — statistics, unique insights, and expert perspectives that AI systems prefer to reference
- Monitor AI search results monthly for hospitality furniture queries to track when DMD begins appearing in generated responses
