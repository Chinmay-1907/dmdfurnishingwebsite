# Skill: geo-proposal (Client-Facing GEO Optimization Proposal) — dmdredesign.netlify.app
**Date:** 2026-04-09 | **Status:** COMPLETE

## Summary
Client-facing GEO optimization proposal for DMD Furnishing outlining a phased approach to achieving AI search visibility. The proposal covers three phases over six months, progressing from foundational fixes through entity building to content optimization for citability.

## Proposed Phases

### Phase 1: Foundation Fixes (Month 1)
- Fix broken sameAs links in Organization schema (LinkedIn 404, missing YouTube)
- Create and verify Google Business Profile with complete NAP data
- Create LinkedIn company page with full business profile, services, and project portfolio
- Ensure all on-site structured data is complete and error-free
- Verify llms.txt accuracy and completeness

### Phase 2: Entity Presence Building (Month 2-3)
- Create Wikidata entry for DMD Furnishing with structured property data (industry, location, founding, services)
- Launch YouTube channel with project walkthrough videos and product showcases
- Register on 5-10 hospitality industry directories (HospitalityNet, Hotel Business, Boutique Design, HD Magazine)
- Build consistent NAP citations across business directories (BBB, industry associations)
- Create Crunchbase profile if applicable
- Pursue mentions in hospitality trade publications through press releases or contributed articles

### Phase 3: Content Optimization for Citability (Month 4-6)
- Rewrite product descriptions with unique specifications, materials data, and use-case narratives (300+ words each)
- Create FAQ content targeting questions AI systems receive about hospitality furniture
- Develop case studies with quantitative project data (room counts, timelines, budgets) that AI systems can cite
- Build "Expert Insights" content section with industry perspectives and trend analysis
- Implement HowTo and FAQ schema on relevant content pages
- Create comparison content (material guides, style guides) that AI systems use for recommendation queries

## Key Findings
- DMD's technical foundation means Phase 1 is lightweight — mostly creating external profiles and fixing broken links
- Phase 2 is the highest-impact investment — entity presence is the primary blocker to AI citations
- Phase 3 converts existing traffic into authority — content depth drives repeated citations once entity is established
- Expected timeline to first AI search appearance: 2-3 months after Phase 2 completion

## Recommendations
- Begin Phase 1 immediately — all tasks can be completed within one week
- Phase 2 requires consistent effort over 8 weeks — assign a dedicated team member for directory and profile management
- Phase 3 content should be written by someone with hospitality furniture domain expertise to ensure authenticity
- Track progress by querying AI platforms monthly for "hospitality furniture manufacturer" and related terms
