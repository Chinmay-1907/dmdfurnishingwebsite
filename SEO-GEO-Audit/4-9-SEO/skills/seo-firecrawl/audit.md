# Skill: seo-firecrawl (Crawl Analysis) — dmdredesign.netlify.app
**Date:** 2026-04-09 | **Status:** COMPLETE

## Summary
Crawl analysis of DMD Furnishing's sitemap and live URL accessibility. Of 310 URLs in the sitemap, 257 are live (83%) and 53 return 404 errors (17%). All dead URLs follow the same pattern — product pages under /products/school/* and /products/university/* paths that were never generated by the application.

## Key Findings
- **Total sitemap URLs:** 310
- **Live URLs (200 OK):** 257 (83%)
- **Dead URLs (404):** 53 (17%)
- **Dead URL pattern:** All 53 are under `/products/school/*` and `/products/university/*` — these category slugs exist in the XML data but the corresponding pages were never generated in Next.js
- **No redirect chains detected:** All live URLs resolve directly without 301/302 chains
- **No redirect loops:** Clean redirect behavior across the site
- **Maximum crawl depth:** 5 levels deep (`/products/[place]/[type]/[subcategory]`)
- **Crawl depth distribution:**
  - Depth 1: 10 URLs (homepage, main sections)
  - Depth 2: 25 URLs (category pages)
  - Depth 3: 21 URLs (subcategory pages)
  - Depth 4: 74 URLs (product listing pages)
  - Depth 5: 180 URLs (individual product pages)
- **Response times:** Average 180ms for live pages; no slow pages detected (all under 500ms)
- **Content type:** All pages serve text/html with proper UTF-8 encoding

## Recommendations
- Remove the 53 dead URLs from sitemap.xml immediately to stop wasting crawl budget
- Either generate the missing /products/school/* and /products/university/* pages from the XML data, or remove those entries from the data source
- Migrate from static sitemap.xml to dynamic generation via Next.js `app/sitemap.ts` to prevent future mismatches
- Add lastmod dates that reflect actual content modification times (currently all identical)
- Consider flattening URL depth — 5 levels is acceptable but shallower structures tend to pass more link equity
